The 4-tier framework
#YourBrand Branded (1–3 per post)
Unique to you. Builds community identity, tracks UGC, and protects your content space. Always include at least one.
#Fitness High-volume 1M+ (2–3 per post)
Broad reach, high competition. Use for algorithmic signal — they rarely drive discovery but tell the algorithm what category you're in.
#HomeWorkout2024 Mid-tier 50K–1M (5–8 per post)
Your primary growth engine. Real engagement with real audiences. Rotate these regularly to avoid saturation.
#KettlebellMoms Niche under 50K (3–5 per post)
Hyper-targeted, high quality audience. Lower competition means you can rank and stay ranked for longer.
Platform placement rules
Instagram
20–30 tags · First comment preferred · Mix all 4 tiers · Avoid banned tags
TikTok
3–6 tags in caption · Trending + 1 niche · Keep caption clean and readable
YouTube Shorts
3–5 tags in description · Focus on topic keywords · No first-comment trick
LinkedIn
3–5 tags · End of post only · Professional niche tags · No generic broad tags
X / Twitter
1–2 tags max · Inline or end · Trending only · More tags = lower engagement
Facebook
2–3 tags · Minimal · Broad reach only · Tags matter less here
Rotation & refresh strategy
Rotate 30–50% of your mid-tier and niche hashtags every 2–3 weeks to prevent algorithmic fatigue. Keep branded and high-volume tags consistent as anchors. Monitor which tags drive the most reach in your analytics and double down on those clusters. Emerging hashtags (under 10K posts) are goldmines — get in early before they saturate.
